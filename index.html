Background of the trip

On 10th December, 2023, me and eighteen other students from Residential College 4 (RC4) at the National University of Singapore embarked on a 12-day overseas trip to Indonesia. Our objective? To dive deep into understanding how land policies affect water governance. Our focus was on two vital Indonesian rivers: the Kali Code in central Yogyakarta and the Kali Ciliwung in Jakarta.

Teaming up with Universitas Gadjah Mada (UGM), we set out a clear mission to uncover the intricate relationship between land policies and the management of water resources in Indonesia. We spent our days deeply engaged, interacting with locals, policymakers, and experts intimately familiar with the complexities of water governance.

We explored how housing policies shaped lives, observed the myriad ways water was used in these bustling towns, and dived into conversations about Normalisation and Naturalisation - ways to restore and integrate rivers into Indonesian life.

Our discussions were not just academic. They were rooted in the stories and dreams of those directly impacted by these policies. The knowledge we acquired was truly invaluable.

A massive shout-out goes to our incredible hosts, the inspiring professors, and the dedicated facilitators who made this eye-opening journey possible. I can't thank Navarun Varma, Ph.D., and the amazing minds at Universitas Gadjah Mada (UGM) enough for guiding us every step of the way.

This trip was not just about academic exploration. It was about envisioning a future where these lesser-known regions thrive sustainably, becoming models of harmonious coexistence with nature.
