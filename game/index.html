    Game Based Model

    To vividly illustrate the challenges, we propose the creation of a game that immerses players in the complexities of water governance in river communities. As we have identified the potential solution of a centralised government, the implementation of our sequential relocation and clean-up can be its focus on how to best relocate settlers. Through strategic thinking and problem-solving, players navigate the management of a river ecosystem, addressing pollution, and handling community resistance to relocation. This interactive game serves as a tool to raise awareness and promote understanding of sustainable solutions for river ecosystems.

    Ideas that we have thought of for the game can be a simulation where the 3 local governments are played by 3 players while a central government played by another player. The game can aim to simulate the relocation of various stakeholders such as settlers and corporations while exploring the relationships between these groups. The interaction between these groups represented as pieces can have direct or inverse relationships with each other and represents factors such as economic growth or stress migration thereby deteriorating the environment and reducing monetary earnings. With this, we will firstly explore relevant CLDs and SFDs that will help us explore these relationships.
